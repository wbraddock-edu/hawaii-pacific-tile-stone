Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about tile and stone work on Kauai.

Both are excellent for Hawaii's conditions, but they have different characteristics. Porcelain tile is highly uniform, water-resistant, available in many styles (including realistic stone looks), and generally lower maintenance. Natural stone — travertine, slate, basalt — has unique character that tile can't replicate, but requires sealing and more careful cleaning. During our free consultation, we'll help you evaluate which makes more sense for your specific space, usage, and aesthetic goals.
A typical bathroom tile project takes 3–7 days depending on size and complexity. This includes demo (if needed), waterproofing, tile setting, grouting, and sealing. We allow the appropriate cure time between phases — rushing these steps is a common cause of future failures. We'll give you a specific schedule during the quoting process.
We can supply tile and stone, or install material you've purchased separately. If you supply your own material, we'll review it before installation to ensure it's appropriate for your application. We have access to premium tile and stone from suppliers that aren't available at retail stores, so our supply-and-install packages often offer good value.

In Hawaii's humid environment, we recommend sealing natural stone annually for high-traffic areas and every 2 years for lower-traffic spaces. Unsealed natural stone in Hawaii can absorb moisture and staining agents much faster than in drier climates. We offer an annual sealing maintenance service starting at $250 that includes inspection and resealing.
For outdoor areas exposed to Kauai's rain, sun, and humidity, we recommend: porcelain with a slip-resistant finish (minimum R10 slip rating) for areas near water; natural basalt or slate for dryer areas where natural character is preferred; and concrete pavers for driveways and high-traffic walkways. We never install polished stone outdoors in Hawaii — it becomes dangerously slippery when wet.
Matching existing tile is one of the most challenging aspects of repair work. Tile styles change, dye lots vary, and discontinued lines can be impossible to source. We'll make every effort to find a match, and if an exact match isn't available, we'll propose creative solutions — like a contrasting accent pattern or blending in the repair decoratively. We're always honest about what's achievable before starting.
